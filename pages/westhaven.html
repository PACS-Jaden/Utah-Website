About West Haven, Utah

General Information

West Haven is a rapidly growing city located in Weber County, Utah, just west of Ogden. It's known for its family-friendly environment, beautiful parks, and proximity to both city and outdoor activities.

Historical Overview

West Haven was officially incorporated in 1991 as a merge between the communities of Kanesville and Wilson. Since then, it has become one of the fastest-growing cities in Northern Utah, attracting families and professionals alike.

Economy & Demographics

Economic Overview

West Haven is a primarily residential city, with a growing business sector that supports local services and retail. The city's economy benefits from its proximity to Ogden and Salt Lake City, making it a desirable location for commuters.

Demographics

  • Median Age: 34.7 years
  • Median Household Income: $68,000
  • Ethnic Diversity: 80% White, 10% Hispanic or Latino, 5% Asian, 5% Other
  • Education: Over 35% of residents hold a bachelor's degree or higher

Tourism & Culture

West Haven offers numerous parks and recreational activities for families. The city's trail system and community events, like the annual West Haven Days celebration, provide a vibrant cultural experience for residents.
