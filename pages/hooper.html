About Hooper, Utah

General Information

Hooper is a city in Weber County, Utah, United States, located about 30 miles north of Salt Lake City. It is a growing residential community with a rich agricultural history.

Key Facts
Attribute Details

Historical Overview

Founded in the late 19th century, Hooper was primarily an agricultural town. It has since transitioned into a suburban community, while still maintaining its rural charm and agricultural roots.

Economy & Demographics

Economic Overview

Hooper's economy is driven by agriculture, local businesses, and a growing residential sector. The city's proximity to Ogden and Salt Lake City provides residents with access to a range of employment opportunities.

Demographics

  • Median Age: 35.4 years
  • Median Household Income: $72,000
  • Ethnic Diversity: 90% White, 6% Hispanic or Latino, 2% Asian, 2% Other
  • Education: Over 30% of residents hold a bachelor's degree or higher

Tourism & Culture

Hooper is known for its rural charm and annual events like the Tomato Days festival. The city offers easy access to outdoor recreation in the nearby Great Salt Lake and the Wasatch Mountains.
